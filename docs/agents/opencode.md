# OpenCode

OpenCode is worth treating as a serious hosted fallback when local models are not enough.

## Why It Matters In This Stack

- stronger hosted coding quality than many small local models
- useful for CPU-only setups or difficult tasks that exceed low-VRAM hardware
- practical when you want better instruction following without rebuilding your whole workflow

## Where It Fits

- CPU-only primary workflow
- fallback for difficult refactors
- backup when local models fail on formatting or tool obedience

## Related Notes

- OpenCode is a good free-tier or low-friction option to evaluate depending on your account access
- It pairs well with the same workflow mindset used in Aider, Hermes, and Pi
- You can keep local Ollama models for privacy/offline use and use OpenCode only when needed
